Building a H-fractal:

Construction starts with a unit square. First step: paint
a 1/2 square in the center with white. Then you need to
mentally divide the square into 4 identical squares and
paint a square with a side of 1/4 in the center of each
of them. Further, each of these 4 squares will again
turn out into 4 parts, a total of 16 squares, and with
each of them you need to do the same. Etc.

It all starts with a figure in the shape of the letter H,
in which the vertical and horizontal segments are
equal. Then, to each of the 4 ends of the figure, a copy
is drawn, reduced in half. To each end (there are
already 16 of them) a copy of the letter H is added,
already reduced by 4 times. Etc. In the limit, a fractal
will turn out, which visually almost fills a certain
square.

H-fractal properties:

The fractal dimension of the part painted in white in
the end is equal to log24 = 2. It is everywhere dense in
the original square. This means that no matter what
point of the square we take, in any, no matter how
small, its neighborhood there will be filled points. That
is, as a result, almost everything became white - the
area of the remainder is equal to 0, and the fractal
occupies an area of 1. But the length of the border of
the filled part is infinite.

The H-fractal is dense everywhere in it. That is, in any
neighborhood of any point of the square, there are
fractal points. Very similar to what happens with the
T-square. This is not accidental, because if you look
closely, you can see that each letter H is contained
in its own small square, which was completed
at the same step. We can say that the H-fractal
fills its own square (space-filling curve).
Therefore, its fractal dimension is 2.

Information about Color Models

RGB

HSV

The RGB color model is an additive color model in
which the red, green, and blue primary colors of light
are added together in various ways to reproduce a
broad array of colors. The name of the model comes
from the initials of the three additive primary colors,
red, green, and blue.The color is encoded by the
gradations of the component channels. As the
magnitude of the gradation of a channel increases,
its intensity increases during synthesis.

RGB is a device-dependent color model: different
devices detect or reproduce a given RGB value
differently, since the color elements (such as phosphors
or dyes) and their response to the individual red, green,
and blue levels vary from manufacturer to
manufacturer, or even in the same device over time.
Thus an RGB value does not define the same color
across devices without some kind of color management.

HSV is an alternative representation of the RGB color
model developed in the 1970s by computer graphics
researchers to more closely match how human vision
perceives color-generating attributes. In these models,
the colors of each shade are arranged in a radial cut
around the central axis of neutral colors, which ranges
from black at the bottom to white at the top.

Hue - color tone (for example, red, green or blue).
Varies in the range of 0-360Â°, but is sometimes
reduced to the range 0-100 or 0-1.
Saturation - saturation. Varies between 0-100 or 0-1.
The larger this parameter, the "purer" the color, so
this parameter is sometimes called color purity. And
the closer this parameter is to zero, the
closer the color is to neutral gray.
Value - the value of color, or Brightness - brightness.
Also set in the range 0-100 or 0-1.

Information about Affine Transormation

An affine transformation is any transformation that preserves collinearity (i.e., all points lying on a line initially still lie on the line after transformation) and distance ratios (for example, the midpoint of a line segment remains the midpoint after transformation). In this sense, affinity indicates a special class of projective transformations that do not move any objects from the affine space R ^ 3 to the plane at infinity or vice versa. Affine transformation is also called affinity.

Geometric contraction, expansion, expansion, reflection, rotation, shear, similarity transformations, spiral similarity and translation are all affine transformations, as well as their combinations. In general, an affine transform is a combination of rotations, translations, stretches, and translations.

Although the affine transform preserves the aspect ratio of the lines, it does not necessarily preserve the angles or length. Any triangle can be transformed into any other using an affine transformation, therefore all triangles are affine and in this sense are affine generalizations of congruent and similar ones.

A particular example of combining rotation and expansion is the rotation-enlargement transformation.
